Our evaluation categories

Robot vacuums can look similar on spec sheets. The differences that matter show up in mapping reliability, pickup performance, edge behavior, and ongoing hassle.

Category What we look for Typical red flags
Navigation & mapping Room coverage, obstacle avoidance, reliability over repeated runs Missed rooms, repeated bumps, frequent “lost” behavior
Pickup (hard floor) Fine dust, crumbs, edge pickup, consistency Scatter, streaky pickup, weak edge behavior
Pickup (carpet) Pet hair handling, deep pickup, brush design Hair tangles, poor agitation, clogged intake
Mopping Stain reduction, edge reach, water control Over-wetting, poor pad contact, weak edge reach
Usability App stability, schedules, room controls, no-go zones Buggy maps, confusing controls, unreliable schedules
Maintenance Brush tangles, bin emptying, filter access, parts Frequent clogs, messy emptying, hard-to-find consumables
Noise Perceived loudness, tone, “annoying” frequencies High-pitched whine, loud auto-empty station

How we choose “top picks”

Our “best overall” recommendation usually balances navigation reliability, strong pickup on typical floors, reasonable maintenance, and a stable app experience. Budget picks emphasize value and low ongoing hassle. Premium picks must meaningfully improve day-to-day outcomes, not just add features on paper.

What earns a recommendation

  • Reliable mapping across multiple runs
  • Consistent pickup without frequent babysitting
  • Maintenance that doesn’t become a weekly headache
  • Controls that work (zones, schedules, room selection)

What can disqualify a model

  • Frequent stuck events or unreliable maps
  • Hair tangles that require constant manual cleanup
  • Mopping that over-wets or barely changes outcomes
  • App issues that break day-to-day use

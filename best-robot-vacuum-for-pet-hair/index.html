Quick decision: what matters for pet hair

The 60-second checklist

Prioritize these

  • Brush design: rubber/anti-tangle systems reduce hair wrap.
  • Pickup consistency: especially at edges and along baseboards.
  • Filtration: better filters help with fine dander and dust.
  • Dock convenience: pets fill bins fast, docks keep you consistent.

Common watch-outs

  • Tangles: long hair can still wrap around axles and side brushes.
  • Clogs: thick fur + litter can clog narrow intakes on some models.
  • Maintenance reality: docks reduce emptying, not brush checks.
  • Rugs: shag and fringe still confuse many robots.

FAQ

Quick answers, no fluff
What makes a robot vacuum good for pet hair?
Brush design matters most. Anti-tangle or rubber brush systems reduce hair wrapping, while strong pickup and decent filtration keep fur and dander under control. Low-hassle maintenance matters because pet homes fill bins and clog brushes faster.
Do I need a self-emptying dock for pets?
For most pet homes, yes. A dock reduces how often you handle debris and helps the robot stay consistent when you run it daily. If you have one pet and a small space, you can skip the dock, but expect more frequent emptying.
Are robot vacuums good for cat litter and tracked grit?
Better models handle litter and grit well on hard floors, but you’ll get the best results with frequent runs and regular brush/filter checks. In Canada, winter grit and salt dust can add extra wear, so maintenance consistency matters.
How often should I maintain a robot vacuum in a pet home?
Plan for quick weekly checks: clear hair from brushes/rollers, wipe sensors, and clean the filter. In heavier shedding seasons, you may need to do brush checks more often. Docks reduce emptying but don’t eliminate brush maintenance.

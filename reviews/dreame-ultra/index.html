Dreame Ultra review (2026)

This is the “high-capability” lane: strong all-around cleaning with a bigger focus on hair handling and dock convenience. The key question is whether you’ll actually maintain the dock and consumables. If yes, ownership feels easier.

Last updated: 2026-02-03

Verdict

Best for: pet hair, mixed floors, people who want fewer “daily chores.” Skip if: you don’t want dock upkeep (water/filters/pads) or you want the cheapest reliable option.

Pros

  • Strong everyday pickup, especially on hard floors
  • Better hair handling than many “generic” bots
  • Dock reduces routine friction (when maintained)
  • Good room targeting, schedules, and zone control

Cons

  • Dock + consumables add ongoing upkeep
  • Price can be high depending on bundle/seller
  • Mopping helps daily grime, not deep stains

Performance breakdown

  • Navigation: generally reliable in open layouts; clutter still matters for any robot.
  • Pickup: strong day-to-day results across crumbs, dust, and tracked-in debris.
  • Pet hair: fewer tangles if you keep the brush clean and run it frequently.
  • Mopping: best used as maintenance cleaning; set expectations for sticky messes.
  • Noise: normal while cleaning; dock emptying is louder for short bursts.
  • Maintenance: brush checks + filter care; dock needs periodic cleaning.

How we evaluate

We focus on navigation reliability, pickup (hard floor + carpet), mopping outcomes, usability, noise, and maintenance. Full rubric: How We Test. If you’re deciding between styles of bots, use How to Choose.

FAQ

Is this a good choice for pet hair?
Yes, especially if you run it often and do quick brush checks. Pet homes win when maintenance stays consistent.
Does the dock mean “zero maintenance”?
Not zero. It reduces daily chores, but you still need occasional dock cleaning and consumable replacement.
Should I buy this or a value pick?
If budget matters most, value picks are safer. If you want lower weekly friction and will maintain a dock, this lane can feel better long-term.

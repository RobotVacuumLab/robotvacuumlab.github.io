FAQ

Quick answers, no fluff
Are robot vacuums worth it for Canadian homes?
For most homes, yes if you value convenience. The biggest win is consistent upkeep, especially with pets, kids, or high-traffic floors. The best results come from reliable navigation, decent pickup, and low-hassle maintenance (bins, filters, brushes).
What matters more: suction power or navigation?
Navigation usually matters more day-to-day. A robot that maps reliably and doesn’t get lost will clean more total area. Suction helps on carpets, but consistent coverage and obstacle handling often determine whether it actually finishes the job.
Do I need a self-emptying dock?
If you have pets, carpet, or you run daily, a dock is a major quality-of-life upgrade. It reduces how often you touch dust and debris, and helps the robot stay consistent. For small apartments with light mess, a dock is optional.
How often do I need to maintain a robot vacuum?
Expect quick weekly basics: emptying (or changing bags), clearing hair from the brush, and wiping sensors. Filters usually need periodic cleaning/replacement. Maintenance is why low-hassle designs score higher in our picks.
Can robot vacuums handle rugs, thresholds, and winter grit?
Better models handle rug edges and typical thresholds more reliably, but thick shag and tall transitions can still be tricky. Winter grit and salt dust are common in Canada, so frequent runs and regular brush/filter checks make a noticeable difference.
Why do prices change so often on Amazon?
Robot vacuums fluctuate with promos, inventory, and bundles. That’s why we use “Check price” buttons instead of hard-coding prices, so you always see the current deal at click time.

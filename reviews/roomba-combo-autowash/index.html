Roomba Combo AutoWash review (2026)

This model is for people who want the convenience story: vacuum + mop routines with less daily effort. The trade-off is that “hands-off” still requires periodic station upkeep, and performance depends on your floors and clutter.

Last updated: 2026-02-03

Verdict

Best for: mostly hard floors, regular schedules, people who value an established ecosystem and service support. Skip if: you need top-tier deep carpet pickup or you don’t want to maintain a wash/empty station.

Pros

  • Convenience-first workflow for vacuum + mop routines
  • Good daily hard-floor upkeep when run frequently
  • Strong “set a schedule and forget it” experience
  • Established brand ecosystem and support footprint

Cons

  • Station upkeep still exists (wash system, tanks, filters)
  • Carpet performance can vary by home and settings
  • Price can be high versus value picks

Performance breakdown

  • Navigation: best results in moderately tidy homes; robots dislike cables and small clutter.
  • Pickup: good daily debris control; carpet depth-cleaning may need extra passes.
  • Mopping: strongest for light daily footprints and dust, not sticky spills.
  • Noise: normal cleaning volume; station cycles are louder for short bursts.
  • Maintenance: brush checks + periodic station cleaning, plus consumables.

FAQ

Is the “AutoWash” station truly hands-free?
It reduces daily chores, but you still need to clean parts, manage water/tanks (if applicable), and replace consumables over time.
Is this better than a value pick?
If your priority is convenience and you’ll maintain the station, it can feel better week-to-week. If budget is priority #1, a value pick is usually the safer move.
Who should skip this model?
Homes with lots of deep carpet or those who don’t want station upkeep will often be happier with a simpler vacuum-first robot.
